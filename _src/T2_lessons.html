Lesson 1 · Ratio and Proportion NA 6NA-IIa-1

Subject MatterExpressing/simplifying ratios; finding the missing term in a proportion
Time2 sessions (100 min)
Materialscolored counters, recipe cards, map with scale
Learning Objectives
(K) define ratio and proportion and state the cross-products rule; (S) express/simplify ratios and solve for a missing term; (V) value fairness and balance.
Activity — “Sago’t Gulaman.” Groups mix “drinks” using 2 cups syrup to 5 cups water and describe the relationship between the amounts.
Analysis — How do we write “2 to 5”? If we double the recipe, how much of each? Does the taste (relationship) change?
Abstraction — A ratio compares two quantities (2:5 or 25); simplify like fractions. A proportion states two equal ratios; solve a missing term by cross-multiplication: 25 = n15 → 5n = 30 → n = 6.
Application — “3 notebooks cost ₱75. How much do 8 notebooks cost?” (Answer: 375=8n → n = ₱200.)
Formative Assessment / Assignment
3-item exit pass. Assignment: find a ratio in your kitchen recipe.

Lesson 2 · Direct and Partitive Proportion NA 6NA-IIb-2

Subject MatterSolving problems involving direct and partitive proportion
Time2 sessions (100 min)
Materialsplay money, sharing cards, chart paper
Learning Objectives
(K) distinguish direct from partitive proportion; (S) solve real-life proportion problems; (V) show fairness in sharing.
Activity — “Sharing the Harvest.” Divide ₱600 among 3 children in the ratio 1:2:3 using play money.
Analysis — When more workers means more pay, what kind of relationship is it (direct)? When one amount is split into parts (partitive), how do we find each share?
Abstraction — Direct: as one quantity increases, the other increases proportionally. Partitive: a whole is divided into parts following a ratio. For 1:2:3, total parts = 6; ₱600 ÷ 6 = ₱100/part → ₱100, ₱200, ₱300.
Application — “5 kg of rice costs ₱250. How much do 12 kg cost?” (Answer: ₱50/kg → ₱600.)
Formative Assessment / Assignment
Pair board work. Assignment: share ₱120 between two siblings in ratio 2:3.

Lesson 3 · Percent, Rate, and Base NA 6NA-IIc-3

Subject MatterRelating percent to fractions/decimals; finding percentage, rate, and base
Time2 sessions (100 min)
Materials10×10 grids, sale tags, mini Techan’s triangle
Learning Objectives
(K) convert among percent, fractions, and decimals; (S) find percentage, rate, or base; (V) value wise spending.
Activity — “Color the Grid.” On a 10×10 grid learners shade 25 squares and express it as 25%, 0.25, and 14.
Analysis — What does “percent” mean (per hundred)? In “20% of 50 is 10,” which is the rate, base, percentage?
Abstraction — Percentage = Rate × Base. Rate is the percent, Base is the whole, Percentage is the part. Example: 15% of 200 = 0.15 × 200 = 30. To find rate: 30 is what % of 200 → 30÷200 = 15%.
Application — “A class of 40 has 30 present. What percent are present?” (Answer: 30÷40 = 75%.)
Formative Assessment / Assignment
3-item quiz card. Assignment: write 3 percents you see in stores as fractions.

Lesson 4 · Percent in Daily Life NA 6NA-IId-4

Subject MatterDiscount, commission, sales tax, and simple interest
Time2 sessions (100 min)
Materialssale flyers, mock bank slips, calculators (optional)
Learning Objectives
(K) define discount, commission, sales tax, simple interest; (S) solve related percent problems; (V) become a smart consumer and saver.
Activity — “Sale sa Mall!” Groups compute the sale price of items marked “20% off.”
Analysis — Is the discount added or subtracted? How is commission like a reward for selling? Why does the government collect sales tax?
Abstraction — Discount = rate × original; sale price = original − discount. Commission = rate × sales. Sales tax = rate × price (added). Simple Interest I = P × R × T.
Application — “A ₱1,500 bag is 20% off. Sale price?” (Discount ₱300 → ₱1,200.) “₱5,000 saved at 3% per year for 2 years earns how much interest?” (I = 5000×0.03×2 = ₱300.)
Formative Assessment / Assignment
Real-flyer task. Assignment: compute interest on ₱2,000 at 5% for 1 year.

Lesson 5 · Angles — Right, Acute, Obtuse MG 6MG-IIe-5

Subject MatterClassifying and measuring angles
Time1–2 sessions (50–100 min)
Materialsprotractors, paper fasteners + strips (angle makers), objects in the room
Learning Objectives
(K) classify angles as acute (<90°), right (=90°), obtuse (>90°); (S) measure angles with a protractor; (V) appreciate accuracy.
Activity — “Angle Hunt.” Learners find and sketch angles around the room (book corner, open door, clock hands).
Analysis — Which angles look “square”? Smaller? Wider? How can we measure exactly?
Abstraction — Demonstrate protractor use (align vertex and base line, read the correct scale). Acute < 90°; Right = 90°; Obtuse between 90° and 180°; Straight = 180°.
Application — Measure and classify 4 given angles; draw a 45°, a 90°, and a 120° angle.
Formative Assessment / Assignment
Classify-5-angles slip. Assignment: find 3 angles at home and classify them.

Lesson 6 · Transformations & Volume MG 6MG-IIf-6 / 6MG-IIg-7

Subject MatterTranslation, reflection, rotation; converting volume/capacity units; volume of cubes & rectangular prisms
Time2 sessions (100 min)
Materialscut-out shapes, mirror, grid paper, unit cubes, boxes, measuring cups
Learning Objectives
(K) describe the effect of slides, flips, and turns; state V = l×w×h and V = s³; (S) identify transformations and compute volume; convert mL↔L and cm³↔L; (V) value spatial care.
Activity — “Slide, Flip, Turn.” Learners move a cut-out shape on grid paper and name each move; then fill a box with unit cubes and count.
Analysis — Does the shape change size when slid/flipped/turned? How many cubes fit in one layer × how many layers?
Abstraction — Translation = slide, reflection = flip (mirror image), rotation = turn about a point; size/shape stay the same. Volume: rectangular prism V = l×w×h; cube V = s³. 1 L = 1000 mL = 1000 cm³.
Application — “A box is 8 cm × 5 cm × 4 cm. Find its volume.” (V = 160 cm³.) “A cube has edge 3 cm.” (V = 27 cm³.)
Formative Assessment / Assignment
Identify-the-transformation + 2 volume items. Assignment: find the volume of a box at home.
