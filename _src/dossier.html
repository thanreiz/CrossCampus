1. What is the MATATAG Curriculum?

MATATAG (a Filipino word meaning “sturdy / resilient”) is the Department of Education’s recalibration of the K–10 Basic Education Program, launched on 10 August 2023. It decongests the old K–12 curriculum, sharpens focus on foundational skills (literacy and numeracy), and strengthens values and 21st-century competencies. It is rolled out in phases:

School YearGrades implementing MATATAG
2024–2025Kindergarten, Grade 1, Grade 4, Grade 7
2025–2026Grade 2, Grade 5, Grade 8
2026–2027Grade 3, Grade 6, Grade 9
2027–2028Grade 10 (full K–10 coverage)
Why this matters for Grade 6
Grade 6 enters the MATATAG curriculum in SY 2026–2027. Teachers are expected to use MATATAG-aligned Lesson Exemplars and the three-term Budget of Work — not the old MELC-based DLL format. Assessment also shifts from four quarters to three terms (trimesters).

2. The Grade 6 Mathematics Framework

MATATAG Mathematics is built on three content domains (replacing the older five strands), wrapped by the Big Ideas of the discipline and developed through the mathematical skills and processes (communicating, reasoning, representing, connecting, modelling, problem-solving). Grade 6 is the final year of Key Stage 2 (Grades 4–6).

CodeContent DomainFocus at Grade 6
NANumber & Algebra Decimals, fractions, ratio & proportion, percentages, exponents & GEMDAS, GCF & LCM
MGMeasurement & Geometry Angles, tessellation, transformations, volume & capacity, plane figures, circles, area & perimeter
DPData & Probability Pie graphs, data interpretation, measures of central tendency, simple experimental probability
